Where is Rangers Valley? A Story Rooted in Precision and Passion

Rangers Valley is nestled in the pristine highlands of Glen Innes, New South Wales, Australia. This location isn’t just picturesque—it’s integral to their success. The cool climate and fertile pastures create the ideal environment for raising their cattle with care and precision. What sets Rangers Valley apart is their unwavering commitment to producing world-class beef. They specialize in high-marbling Wagyu and Black Angus cattle, raised with a focus on sustainability and quality. This dedication has earned them a reputation as a global leader in premium beef production.

What Makes Rangers Valley Beef Exceptional?


Rangers Valley beef is all about quality. The cattle are raised on a carefully managed diet to ensure exceptional marbling, tenderness, and flavor. Their flagship product, Rangers Valley WX, is renowned for its rich, buttery taste and consistent marbling. This level of quality doesn’t happen by chance—it’s the result of meticulous planning and execution. Restaurants and retailers worldwide seek out Rangers Valley beef for its unparalleled consistency and taste. Whether it’s a high-end steakhouse in Tokyo or a boutique butcher shop in New York, the demand remains strong. Premium quality comes with a premium price tag. Rangers Valley Wagyu price reflects the time, effort, and resources invested in every step of their production process. Rangers Valley WX: The Crown Jewel of Wagyu


Rangers Valley WX is more than just a product—it’s a promise. Every cut reflects the brand’s dedication to excellence. Who Owns Rangers Valley?


Rangers Valley is owned by Marubeni Corporation, a Japanese multinational conglomerate. Their ownership brings a unique fusion of Japanese precision and Australian expertise to the brand. This partnership ensures that Rangers Valley remains at the forefront of innovation while maintaining its deep roots in sustainable Australian agriculture.
